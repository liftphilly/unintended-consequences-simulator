Unintended Consequences Simulator

Would S-Corp owners reclassify as LLCs under Bill No. 251026 (the LIFT Act)?

Who would actually switch?

Now that Bill No. 251026 has been confirmed to pass PA constitutional muster, the biggest remaining concern raised by the Law Department about the LIFT Act is that wealthy business owners would dissolve their S-Corporations and reclassify as LLCs to capture the new BIRT exemption, causing revenue losses far beyond projections.

This simulator tests that claim.

It compares the total tax burden of an S-Corp owner against the same business operating as an LLC after the bill passes. The math accounts for federal income tax, state income tax, city wage tax, BIRT, NPT, payroll and self-employment taxes, QBI deductions, and retirement contributions.

The Finding

At these inputs, an S-Corp owner who converts to solo classification would lose $46,030 per year in after-tax income.
At virtually every income level, S-Corp owners pay less tax than LLCs. The advantage comes from federal payroll tax savings that no city bill can change.
In the rare marginal scenarios where LLC edges ahead, the advantage is negligible and far outweighed by the $4,000 - $11,000 in legal and administrative costs of dissolution, plus a 5-year federal lockout from re-electing S-Corp status.
